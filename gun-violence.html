Did you know that there were 4,000 deaths due to gun violence in Texas alone? These numbers have grown 38% since 2011.
Did you know that in 2019 there were 1.299 firearm homicides?
This included 210 children and teen deaths. Which means it is the 27TH highest in the ENTIRE U.S.
So far in the U.S there have been 134 mass shootings all of these claiming the loves of 196 people.
In an average year, 3,647 people die and 5,556 are wounded by guns in Texas.
Texas has the 28th-highest societal cost of gun violence in the US at $1,769 per resident each year.
That is $51.3 BILLION dollars per year ($1.1 billion paid my resident taxes).

CHILDREN AND TEENS GUN DEATHS
Guns are the leading cause of death among children and teens in Texas. In Texas, an average of 372 children and teens die by guns every year.
35% of all gun deaths among children and teens are suicides and 60% are homicides.

INTIMATE PARTNER HOMICIDES
In 2019, 73 women were fatally shot by an intimate partner in Texas. 67% of female intimate partner homicide victims were killed with a gun, compared to 67% nationwide.

DRUG RELATED INSTANSES
68% of gun murders are caused by substance taking. Of all substances, alcohol is the most reported substance taken before the crime commted related to gun violence.
